Tamsui

08/03/2025

A calming port where I could smell the sea very prominently. A bunch of people like to fish here. The food was also great; the mochi here was show-stopping, and its local dish ah gei had everything I liked: glass noodles wrapped in a juicy meat and tofu skin with a sweet-and-spicy sauce. I also loved seeing the history of Taiwan being played out in the forts of Tamsui, once an important military fort defending the northern coast, later a political stronghold as the British embassy and residence, and now a slow fishing port with its own culture.
